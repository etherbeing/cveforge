## CVE Forge Code of Conduct

### Our Pledge

In the interest of fostering an open and welcoming environment, we pledge to make participation in our project and our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

### Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contribute to a positive environment include:

  * Using welcoming and inclusive language.
  * Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences.
  * Accepting constructive criticism gracefully.
  * Focusing on what is best for the community.
  * Showing empathy toward other community members.

Examples of unacceptable behavior by participants include:

  * The use of sexualized language or imagery and unwelcome sexual attention or advances.
  * Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.
  * Public or private harassment.
  * Publishing others' private information, such as physical or electronic addresses, without explicit permission.
  * **Any action that violates the legal and ethical guidelines of the project, specifically: promoting or sharing illegal hacking activities, unauthorized system access, or offensive malware.**

### Scope

This Code of Conduct applies both within project spaces and in public spaces when an individual is representing the project or its community.

### Enforcement

Violations of the Code of Conduct can be reported by contacting the project team at `[your-contact-email-or-team-alias]`. All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

The project team has the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct, or to ban temporarily or permanently any contributor for other behaviors that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.
